About

I aim to create art that resonates with people’s sense of identity, unrestricted by massive, overarching systems that come with living in a society. Currently, I am inspired to create pieces that highlight individual and personal limitations, either through people's artistic expression or recounting lived experiences of restriction. In a similar vein, my work is generally critical of institutions which inhibit human expression, such as large for-profit corporations, government abuses of power, and arbitrary social standards. Through this work, I want viewers to acknowledge how societal systems silently control individuality and expression.
In my workflow, I tend to lay out my ideas and then draw cohesive through-lines to connect those ideas. By using digital mediums, the through-lines can be quickly and constantly modified as new thoughts or inspirations are added to the piece. I want to focus on making the process as free and streamlined as possible, so I try not to restrain myself with any strict standards, especially standards rooted in a larger, more powerful system. Hopefully, I want to demonstrate my own sense of self through my artwork and encourage others to truly follow themselves.
