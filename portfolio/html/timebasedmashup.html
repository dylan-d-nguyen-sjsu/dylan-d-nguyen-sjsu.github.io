It's Time to Wake Up

In It’s Time to Wake Up, I attempt to explore the current social and political position of gender expression through a transgender, male-to-female focus; however, I want the work to apply universally to any expression of gender. In order to convey my stance, the central motif of sleep is illustrated through the repetition, sequencing, and timing of sleeping as well as associations with dreams and beds.
For most of the project, content was added first and connections arose as the process continued. The initial and foundational piece of inspiration came from the project’s song which resembled a lullaby. When thinking about media containing sleep and dreams that I already enjoy through YouTube, I found that they often involved authentic, genuine expressions of feminine and trans-feminine identity through the internet. As such, I combined my collection of oneiric game footage, stream clips, music covers, and also historical imagery together to create the effect of peering into a dream. Additionally, the use of realistic media in the latter half creates a divide between a sleeping world of sleep and an awaking world. Consequently, I want my artwork to require labor through making the viewer interact with unfamiliar and occasionally jarring worlds.
It’s Time to Wake Up showcases that refusing to acknowledge the expression of gender obscures the accepting, nurturant selves present within everyone and reinforces strict gender expectations. The virtual avatars depicted in the video dream simultaneously in two ways: they live their dream by presenting femininely but primarily yearn to have their exploration of gender recognized by wider society. Part of this exploration is discovering the individual’s nurturant and maternal side, the ability to care for others on an emotional, interconnected level; yet, discovering profound compassion for others is the dream that is oftentimes repressed. People need others’ input to develop nurturance, so the harmful and distracting sentiments spewed by wider society only restricts this growth. Gender expectations play a significant role, as men are designed to be unfeeling while women are taught to suppress their nurturant side in the professional space. Ultimately, the need to be sensitive towards another is a universal experience. I hope this dream reaches everyone.
